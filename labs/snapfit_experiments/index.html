One of the nice features of snap-fit connections is that you can tune the insertion/removal force bias. That is to say, you can make the part easier to install than it is to remove (or vice versa). This is simply done by changing the angle on the forward and backward side of the snap flexure. In this case, the part is designed to be easier to install than it is to remove.


I repeatedly inserted and removed example snap-fit pieces in the Instron to verify their performance. The results from testing two geometries are shown below.







One unexcpect part of the data is that the maximum force required to remove the part is less than the force required to insert the part. I believe this is due to the unmodeled change in angle.
