Optical Machining

will langford

cba


As a demo for the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland I tested the Shopbot's ability to machine polycarbonate at optical resolution.



Using super fine stepovers of 0.001" and doing a number of finish cuts results in a surface that feels perfectly smooth to the touch but is still optically foggy. By using a series of polishing compounds a dremel tool, that surface can be made (mostly) optically clear.






The surface of the lens reveals what I believe to be the limiting resolution of the machine's z-axis. Although the tool was moved in finishing cuts in the xz-plane and the yz-plane, stepped rings in the xy-plane developed.


This first test is far from showing optimal results. I think refining the polishing steps is likely required to produce proper lenses.
